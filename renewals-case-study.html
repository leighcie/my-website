Turned a Year-Long Stalled Migration Into a 5-Month Win (And Redesigned A Critical Process)

Cross-Functional Leadership Technical Strategy & Execution Process Innovation & Scalability

Context

Connect for Health Colorado processes hundreds of thousands of insurance applications and renewals annually. A stalled microservices migration blocked teams from redesigning the renewals process, which required taking the platform offline for 8 days and intensive manual developer intervention each year.

Problem

  • Stalled Migration: After a year, only a few services were built; cross-team momentum was missing.
  • Unsustainable Renewals Process: Processing took 8 days, involved constant developer monitoring, and caused customer service downtime.

Vision

Enable a fully automated, end-to-end renewals process: fast, reliable, and independent of constant developer intervention—while demonstrating the value of microservices to all teams.

What I Delivered

  • Coordinated 8 teams and 7 product owners to complete the microservices migration in 5 months.
  • Redesigned the renewals process: 8 days → 18 hours, zero downtime, no weekend monitoring.
  • Established documentation and collaboration patterns adopted across the organization.

Execution

  • Diagnosed the root cause: lack of technical understanding and buy-in among product owners.
  • Created momentum through public, cross-team commitments and visible timelines.
  • Prioritized migration work by showing near-term value for renewals redesign.
  • Implemented a new automated pipeline with isolated error handling and asynchronous resolution.

Impact

Processing time 8 days → 18 hours (94% reduction)
Platform availability 1-day downtime (previously 8 days annually)
Developer work Eliminated 24/7 monitoring and weekend shifts
Organizational change Improved cross-team collaboration, documentation culture, and future project efficiency
Customer experience Uninterrupted service during renewals, fewer errors

Why It Matters

This project demonstrates that complex technical initiatives succeed only when organizational dynamics are addressed alongside engineering challenges. By aligning teams, establishing momentum, and linking migration to tangible outcomes, we delivered speed, reliability, and scalability—transforming a previously "stalled" initiative into a reference point for organizational capability.
